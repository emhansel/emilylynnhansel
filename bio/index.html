Bio

Emily Hansel began her dance training in Rochester, Minnesota at Allegro School of Dance and the Minnesota Youth Ballet Academy. She performed with Minneapolis’s Metropolitan Ballet, danced in several projects at the Ritz Theater in Minneapolis, and founded the Summer Choreography Workshop in Rochester. She attended the summer intensives of Alonzo King LINES Ballet, the San Francisco Conservatory of Dance, and the American Ballet Theatre. In 2012, at age 17, Emily was awarded the Homer Avila Scholarship by Alonzo King LINES Ballet.

In 2014, Emily attended the American Dance Festival School where she performed in the original work of choreographer Carl Flink. In 2015, Emily attended the University of South Florida’s Dance in Paris program where she studied under dancers from companies such as Tanztheater Wuppertal (Pina Bausch), Chunky Move (Anouk van Dijk), and Nederlands Dans Theater.

Currently in her third year of study at the University of South Florida, Emily has established herself as a dynamic performer, choreographer, scholar, and leader. She has performed in works by Ohad Naharin, Alonzo King, Robert Moses, Maurice Causey, Bliss Kohlmyer, Paula Nunez, Andrew Carroll, and Jeanne Travers and, in October 2015, she performed at the International Creative Dance Seminar in Beijing, China. Emily’s choreography has been presented multiple times in USF’s annual Student Dance Production among other shows. Emily is currently conducting research for her Honors Thesis, “The Representation of Ideas by Words and Art,” which will culminate in the choreography and performance of a solo in April 2015.

Emily is the President of USF’s Student Dance Production Board, which entails overseeing every aspect of production for the annual student-run and student-choreographed concert among other managerial and organizational responsibilities. She has worked on the TEDxUSF Affairs Team for two years and has held various other leadership positions in the USF community. Emily is on her way to becoming a King O'Neal Scholar and she will graduate in May 2016 as a Provost's Scholar with a BFA in Dance Performance and an honors degree.
